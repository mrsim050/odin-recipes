Haleem

Description

Haleem (also called daleem, halim, etc.) is a Pakistani, Indian and/or Hyderabadi thick stew made with various grains, lentils (dal/daal), and meat.

Ingredients

  • Rolled Oats
  • Basmati Rice
  • Masoor Dal (Red Lentils)
  • Urad (Mash) Dal
  • Split Chana Dal (split chickpeas)
  • Moong Dal (sometimes called Yellow Lentils)
  • Meat (chicken/beef)
  • Onions
  • Tomatoes
  • Traditional whole and ground spices.

Steps

Here's the plain and simple method broken down into 3 steps to give you an idea of how to make haleem in the Instant Pot.

  1. Soak grains and lentils. Sauté onions, garlic, and ginger. Add the meat, sauté for a bit, then add the rest of the IP ingredients and pressure cook with the Meat/Stew Setting.
  2. Open the Pot. Remove the beef and give it just enough pulses in the food processor to shred. Then use an immersion blender to blend the lentils & grains. If you don't have an immersion blender, run the lentils and grains through the food processor too.
  3. Add oats and cook the haleem down. Once it starts bubbling, cover and slow cook until you can see the 'laced' consistency (or resha). If you're up for it, make a tadka. Garnish and serve!
